**Core Lexical Categories:**

- **Content Words:**
    
    - **Nouns (N):**
        - **Proper Nouns (Nprop):** Specific names of people, places, or things (e.g., John, Paris, Eiffel Tower).
        - **Common Nouns (Ncom):** General categories of people, places, or things (e.g., boy, city, building).
        - **Mass Nouns (Nmass):** Uncountable substances (e.g., water, sugar, happiness).
        - **Count Nouns (Ncount):** Countable objects (e.g., book, apple, person).
    - **Verbs (V):**
        - **Main Verbs (Vmain):** Carry the core action or state (e.g., run, sleep, be).
        - **Auxiliary Verbs (Aux):** Help form tenses, negation, or mood (e.g., be, have, do).
    - **Adjectives (Adj):** Describe nouns or pronouns (e.g., big, red, interesting).
        - **Descriptive Adjectives (AdjDesc):** Provide qualities (e.g., tall, beautiful).
        - **Proper Adjectives (AdjProp):** Derived from proper nouns (e.g., Shakespearean, American).
    - **Adverbs (Adv):** Modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs (e.g., quickly, very, unfortunately).
        - **Manner Adverbs (AdvMan):** Describe how something happens (e.g., quickly, slowly).
        - **Place Adverbs (AdvPla):** Describe location (e.g., here, there, inside).
        - **Time Adverbs (AdvTim):** Describe time (e.g., now, then, always).
        - **Degree Adverbs (AdvDeg):** Modify the intensity of an adjective or adverb (e.g., very, quite, extremely).
- **Function Words:**
    
    - **Determiners (Det):** Specify the noun they precede (e.g., the, a, some).
        - **Articles (DetArt):** Definite (the) and indefinite (a, an).
        - **Demonstratives (DetDem):** Point to specific things (e.g., this, that, these, those).
        - **Quantifiers (DetQuan):** Specify quantity (e.g., some, many, all, few).
        - **Possessives (DetPos):** Indicate ownership (e.g., my, your, his, her).
    - **Prepositions (P):** Show the relationship between a noun or pronoun and another word (e.g., in, on, at).
    - **Conjunctions (Conj):** Connect words, phrases, or clauses (e.g., and, but, because).
        - **Coordinating Conjunctions (ConjCoord):** Join words, phrases, or clauses of equal rank (e.g., and, but, or).
        - **Subordinating Conjunctions (ConjSub):** Introduce dependent clauses (e.g., because, although, since).

**Additional Categories (considered by some theories):**

- **Interjections (Int):** Express emotions or sudden thoughts (e.g., ouch, hello, wow).
- **Pronouns (Pro):** Substitute for nouns (e.g., he, she, it, they).
    - **Personal Pronouns (ProPer):** Refer to specific people or things (e.g., I, you, we, they).
    - **Reflexive Pronouns (ProRef):** Refer back to the subject (e.g., myself, herself, himself).
    - **Possessive Pronouns (ProPos):** Function as possessives (e. g., mine, yours, ours, theirs).
    - **Demonstrative Pronouns (ProDem):** Point to specific things (e.g., this, that, these, those).
    - **Interrogative Pronouns (ProInt):** Used in questions (e.g., who, what, when, where).
    - **Relative Pronouns (ProRel):** Connect clauses (e.g., who, which, that).
- **Quantifiers (Q):** Can function as determiners or adverbs depending on context (e.g., many, much, all, few).
- **Particles (Par):** Small words with specific grammatical functions that vary across languages (e.g., negation particles in some languages).
- **Complementizers (Comp):** These are words that introduce non-finite clauses (e.g., that, whether, for). They are sometimes included under conjunctions but can be a distinct category due to their specific role with non-finite verbs.
