Common adjectives have three forms: regular, comparative, and superlative.

**Illustrations:**
- White (དཀར་པོ།), whiter (དཀར་བ།), whitest (དཀར་ཤོས།)
- Long (རིང་པོ།), longer (རིང་བ།), longest (རིང་ཤོས།)

Superlative works the same as the common adjective.

Comparatives use the [[Tibetan Grammar/The Fifth Case – Origin\|Fifth Case]] (usually the ལས་སྒྲ།) to mark what they are whiter, longer, etc. than. 

**Illustrations:**
- Bigger than that house ཁང་པ་དེ་ལས་ཆེ་བ།
- There are more snow mountains in Tibet than in India བོད་ལ་རྒྱ་གར་ལས་གངས་རི་མང་བ་ཡོད།
