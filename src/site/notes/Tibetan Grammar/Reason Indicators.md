**This category includes the following particles:**
- Third Case particle -ས། (See: [[Tibetan Grammar/The Reason\|here]])
- Fifth Case particle ལས། (See: [[Tibetan Grammar/The Fifth Case – Origin#^cc5c38\|here]])
- The དང་སྒྲ།
- The ཕྱིར་སྒྲ།
- The ན་སྒྲ།

The term རྒྱུ་མཚན། literally means the “signifier of the cause”, reflected in English by “be*cause*”, but having a broader meaning than we immediately think of in terms of reasons. 

In its general sense, it is meant to connect with the verb of the sentence (i.e. an indication of change) and describe what *other than the agent* caused that change (e.g. another verb).

In this way, the reason something happened can be (respectively):
- Because something else happening,
- From something else happening,
- Directly and sequentially connected to something else happening (i.e. when),
- On account of something else happening, or
- If something else happens (conditionally or hypothetically).

However, there are also reasons based on the nature of phenomena rather than their causation. In these cases, neither the reason nor its conclusion have to be verbs and are usually affixed to an [[Tibetan Grammar/Negation and Existence Particles\|Negation or Existence Particle]] (དགག་སྒྲ། སྒྲུབ་སྒྲ།), e.g. ཡིན། མིན། ཡོད། མེད།; this is very common in debate. Since these are not sequential they will not use the ནས་སྒྲ། or the དང་སྒྲ།.

As such, reason statements can have many constructions, for example:
- [[Preliminaries/Tarig/Correct nature sign\|Reason of nature]]:
	- “Since pot is a compounded phenomenon it is impermanent.” བུམ་པ་ནི་འདུ་བྱས་ཡིན་པས་མི་རྟག་པ་ཡིན་ནོ།
	- “Pot is impermanent because it is a compounded phenomenon.” བུམ་པ་ནི་མི་རྟག་པ་ཡིན་ཏེ། འདུ་བྱས་ཡིན་པའི་ཕྱིར།
	- “If pot is a compounded phenomenon, it must be impermanent.” བུམ་པ་ནི་འདུ་བྱས་ཡིན་ན་མི་རྟག་པ་ཡིན་དགོས།
- [[Preliminaries/Tarig/Correct effect sign\|Causal reason]]:
	- “Since I saw a tiger, I got scared.” སྟག་མཐོང་བས་སེམས་ལ་འཇིག་སྐྲག་སྐྱེས།
	- “I got scared from seeing a tiger.” སྟག་མཐོང་བ་ལས་སེམས་ལ་འཇིག་སྐྲག་སྐྱེས།
	- “I saw a tiger and got scared.” སྟག་མཐོང་བ་དང་སེམས་ལ་འཇིག་སྐྲག་སྐྱེས།
	- “I got scared because I saw a tiger.” སེམས་ལ་འཇིག་སྐྲག་སྐྱེས་ཏེ། སྟག་མཐོང་བའི་ཕྱིར།
	- “If I see a tiger, I will get scared.” སྟག་མཐོང་ན་སེམས་ལ་འཇིག་སྐྲག་སྐྱེ།
	- “When I saw a tiger I got scared.” སྟག་མཐོང་བ་ན་སེམས་ལ་འཇིག་སྐྲག་སྐྱེས།

This last example is not explicitly listed as a Reason Indicator, since it is an actual [[Tibetan Grammar/The Time-marker\|Seventh Case time marker]]. However, the meaning applies similar to the དང་སྒྲ། usage in this context. Similarly, the conditional ན་སྒྲ། are only a reason in a looser sense and can be further distinguished through:
- ་་་ཡིན་ན། → ་་་ཡིན་པས་ན།
- ་་་མཐོང་ན། → ་་་མཐོང་བས་ན།

The ཕྱིར་སྒྲ། should not be confused with the [[Tibetan Grammar/The Fourth Case – Purpose\|Fourth Case]] usage, the rule of thumb is:
- As a Fourth Case, it will usually come at the beginning or middle of a sentence
  e.g. “Mind generation is the wish for perfect complete enlightenment for the welfare of others.”
  སེམས་བསྐྱེད་པ་ནི་གཞན་དོན་ཕྱིར། ཡང་དག་རྫོགས་པའི་བྱང་ཆུབ་འདོད།
- As a Reason Indicator, it will usually come at the end
  e.g. “The subject - pot - it is impermanent because it is a product.”
  བུམ་པ་ཆོས་ཅན། མི་རྟག་པ་ཡིན་ཏེ། བྱས་པ་ཡིན་པའི་ཕྱིར།

However, it would remain a Reason Indicator even if restructured as:
- “Due to being a product, pot is impermanent.” བྱས་པ་ཡིན་པའི་ཕྱིར་བུམ་པ་ནི་མི་རྟག་པ་ཡིན།

Moreover, the Reason Indicator ཕྱིར་སྒྲ། will usually link up with a [[Tibetan Grammar/Continuative Particles\|Continuative Particle]] (ལྷག་བཅས།) particle to clearly mark what it is a reason for:
- As in the above: ཡིན་ཏེ། ་་་པའི་ཕྱིར།
