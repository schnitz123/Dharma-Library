The ནི་སྒྲ། is a purely grammatical particle and rarely explicitly makes it into English translation. It has three main usages:
1. Isolation (དགར་བ།)
2. Emphasis (ངེས་བཟུང་ངམ་ནན་ཏན་བརྣན་པ།)
3. Padding (ཚིག་ཁ་སྐོང་།)

The first two are its main usages and thus it is better to think of the ནི་སྒྲ། as a particle that is used to separate out parts of the sentence as being different clauses etc., either for clarification or for emphasis.

**1. Isolation** (དགར་བ།)

This is the most common usage. Usually clarifying a sentence clause in relation to an [[Tibetan Grammar/Negation and Existence Particles\|Existence Particle]] (སྒྲུབ་སྒྲ།) such as ཡིན།. Since ཡིན། takes two things in the [[Tibetan Grammar/The First Case – Nominative\|First Case]] to form the “A is B” construction, it can sometimes be unclear what A and B are (especially as sentences get longer and more complex); the ནི་སྒྲ། is then used to isolate the two.

For example:
- “Pot is impermanent.” བུམ་པ་ནི་མི་རྟག་པ་ཡིན།
- “Being devoid of the eight non-freedoms is freedom.” མི་ཁོམ་པ་བརྒྱད་དང་བྲལ་བ་ནི་དལ་བའོ།
- “Our life runs out very quickly: years run out by the passing months…” 
  ཚེ་འཛད་པ་ཤིན་ཏུ་མྱུར་ཏེ། ལོ་ནི་ཟླ་བ་སོང་བས་འཛད་ཅིང་།

This will also apply to outlines, which are usually marked with the ནི་སྒྲ།, in that, regardless of how long the subsequent text is, it will usually be connected to an explicit or implicit ཡིན།, forming the structure: “The first outline, [xyz], is…[remainder of section].”

For example:
- “The first [outline] – the nature of concentration – is a virtuous single-pointed mind that remains without being distracted elsewhere from its object of observation.”
  དང་པོ་བསམ་གཏན་གྱི་ངོ་བོ་ནི། དམིགས་པ་ལས་གཞན་དུ་མི་གཡེང་བར་གནས་པའི་སེམས་རྩེ་གཅིག་པ་དགེ་བའོ།

Although one would probably *not* translate it as such, this translation shows the literal grammatical structure of the sentence: 
- The outline’s number and name are in [[Tibetan Grammar/Assertion#^c7837b\|apposition]] 
- Together they are marked by the ནི་སྒྲ། as the first element in an implicit ཡིན། statement (due to using the [[Tibetan Grammar/Terminating Particles\|terminating particle]]).
- This creates a structure technically identical to “Pot is impermanent.” བུམ་པ་ནི་མི་རྟག་པ་ཡིན།

**2. Emphasis** (ངེས་བཟུང་ངམ་ནན་ཏན་བརྣན་པ།)

The next usage is for the purposes of emphasis, but often also helps in clarifying clauses. It sets up a word or part of a sentence as important or as the overarching topic or clause of a sentence or paragraph.

For example:
- “*You* are the Lama; *you* are the yidam; *you* are the ḍāka and dharmapāla...” 
  ཁྱོད་ནི་བླ་མ་ཁྱོད་ནི་ཡི་དམ་ཁྱོད་ནི་མཁའ་འགྲོ་ཆོས་སྐྱོང་སྟེ།
- “*If* one has repeatedly contemplated impermanence, the craving hoping not to be separated from these will be overcome.” ཡང་དང་ཡང་དུ་མི་རྟག་པ་བསམས་ན་ནི་དེ་དག་ལ་མི་འབྲལ་བར་རེ་བའི་སྲེད་པ་ལྡོག
- “[It] is established only through the assertion that… [xyz] and not through *anything else*.”
  ་་་ཁས་བླངས་པ་ཁོ་ནས་གྲུབ་པ་ཡིན་གྱི། གཞན་གྱིས་ནི་མ་ཡིན་ནོ།

(The italics are not marking the ནི་སྒྲ། themselves, but rather the words they are emphasising.)

**3. Padding** (ཚིག་ཁ་སྐོང་།)

Finally, this particle will also be used for verse padding when extra syllables are needed for the meter. In doing so, the ནི་སྒྲ། will add no meaning or grammar.
